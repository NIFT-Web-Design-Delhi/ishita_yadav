Kota Doria

Kota Doria Craft originates from Rajasthan, Kota, where artisans have been practicing this craft for generations. The word 'Dori' literally translated from Hindi, means 'threads'. Kethun, a village of about 15,000 people in Rajasthan, is home to the ethereal Kota Doria weave. With 2500 looms, it houses weavers who have been working here for 300 years – their forefathers were brought here from Mysore. ​ The fabric is transparent and light suitable for daily wear throughout the year for Indian people. Over the years the craft has grown its range of products and designs.
